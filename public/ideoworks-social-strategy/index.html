3 Things We're Not Doing (That We Should Be)

📭

No Proof of Results

We post what we do, not what we've achieved. Brands who are deciding whether to hire us need to see real results: numbers, before/afters, what changed. Right now there's none of that.

🫥

No Faces, No Team

No photos of the team, no behind-the-scenes, no personality. People trust people: not logos. When there's no human attached to the work, the work is forgettable.

🔇

No Opinions on the Industry

We share nothing about Indonesian digital marketing trends, no commentary, no takes. The channel reads like a brochure, not a living agency. Competitors who do share opinions get found: we don't.

What Our Potential Clients Are Actually Worried About

🤔

"Does this agency actually deliver?"

Indonesian brand managers have been burned by agencies that pitch well but execute poorly. They want evidence, not claims. Show the numbers.

🎯

"Will they understand our brand?"

They worry a generic playbook gets copy-pasted onto their brand. Content that shows how we think: our process, our decisions: proves we don't do cookie-cutter.

🤝

"Who's actually going to work on our account?"

The person pitching is rarely the person doing the work. Showing team members: by name, with personality: builds trust before the first meeting.

Our One Clear Angle

How We Stand Out

Ideoworks is the agency that shows its work: we share our process, our thinking, and our results, while competitors only post polished outputs.
💡

Why This Works For Us

  • No other local agency owns this lane. Most agencies share portfolio shots and award wins. The "here's how we actually do it" space is wide open.
  • The content team can lead this: process posts, tool walkthroughs, methodology explainers don't require CEO input.
  • One piece of process content does three things at once: proves we know what we're doing, educates potential clients, and builds trust.
  • It works on both platforms: carousels on Instagram and text posts on LinkedIn both reward educational, process-driven content right now.

PILLAR 1: PROOF: Show What We've Achieved

30% of posts
Do This First
Quick win this week: Pick one finished campaign. Write: "We ran a Meta campaign for an e-commerce client during Ramadan 2025. Here's what worked, what didn't, and the one insight that changed our approach." Turn it into a 7-slide LinkedIn carousel using the AI prompt below. Takes 30 minutes. No client name needed.
Why It Works
This is the #1 thing potential clients want to see. They've been burned by agencies before. Numbers: even anonymized ones: prove we actually deliver. Competitors post portfolio shots; we post outcomes. That's the difference.
Where to Get Content
Completed project folders, analytics exports, account managers' monthly reports. You don't need to name the client: industry + numbers + insight is enough.
LinkedIn Posts
• Before/after text post with 3 metrics (reach, cost per lead, conversion)
• PDF carousel: "How we grew an [industry] client's Instagram in 90 days"
• Campaign summary article (anonymized, 600–800 words)
Instagram Posts
• 7-slide carousel: Problem → Insight → Strategy → Execution → Result → What we learned → CTA
• Reel: Before/after split-screen or animated chart reveal (15–30 sec)
• Static graphic: Bold metric over campaign image ("5.2× ROAS in 6 weeks")
AI Prompt
You are a content writer for Ideoworks, a Jakarta-based digital marketing agency. Write a LinkedIn carousel script for this project result: - Industry: [e.g., F&B / Retail / Property] - What we did: [brief description] - Key metric: [e.g., "ROAS 4.1x" or "CPL turun 38%"] - Duration: [weeks/months] Format: 7 slides. Slide 1 = bold hook with the metric. Slides 2–6 = one insight per slide. Slide 7 = CTA to DM or visit ideoworks.id. Tone: confident but not boastful. No client name needed. Each slide max 25 words.
Approval Path
QUICK MANAGER CHECK: Account manager confirms nothing is confidential, then publish. Max 24 hours.

Showing how we think does more than explaining what we do. Potential clients can see the quality of our thinking before we've even met. No faces, no client data: just tools, templates, and process. 100% content-team owned.
Where to Get Content

LinkedIn Posts

PILLAR 3: CULTURE: Show the Team

25% of posts
Do This First
Quick win this week: Pick one team member. Take a photo (or use one from your phone). Ask them 3 questions over WhatsApp: what's their role, what's their superpower, what's one thing most people don't know about the job. Draft a LinkedIn post using the AI prompt below. Why It Works
Clients worry about who's actually on their account. When they see real team members with real personalities before the first meeting, the sales conversation starts warmer. CEO doesn't need to be involved: junior and mid-level team members are the stars here.
Where to Get Content
Team photos, office moments, project wins, brainstorm sessions. Each team member contributes 1 piece of input per month: a quote, a tip, a fun fact: over WhatsApp. That's all you need.
LinkedIn Posts
• Team spotlight: "[Name], our Social Media Analyst: here's how she approaches a new client brief"
• New hire announcement with their first-week learnings
• "3 things our team does that most agencies don't": written by a team lead, not the CEO
Instagram Posts
• Reel: "Meet [Name]: [role]" (30 sec: team member talks to camera OR text-only with their photo)
• Reaction reel: "When the client says 'we love it'": relatable agency humor
• "Our workspace" carousel: office, tools, whiteboard notes, screens
AI Prompt
Write a LinkedIn team spotlight post for a digital marketing agency employee. Details: - Name: [First name only is fine] - Role: [e.g., Content Strategist, Performance Analyst] - Their superpower: [e.g., "finds the right TikTok hook in under 10 minutes"] - One non-work fact: [e.g., "obsessed with Indonesian indie music"] - Time at company: [X months/years] Format: 3 short paragraphs. Para 1: who they are + their superpower. Para 2: their approach to the work (use their own words, paraphrased). Para 3: what clients say / what the team loves about working with them. End with a one-line team tagline. Max 150 words. Warm tone: not HR copy.
Approval Path
POST IT YOURSELF: Team member approves their own spotlight before you publish. That's it.

PILLAR 4: COMMENTARY: Share Our Point of View

20% of posts
Do This First
Quick win this week: Look at one trend in Indonesian digital marketing right now: a Meta update, a TikTok Shop change, something brands are getting wrong. Write a 200-word LinkedIn post with your team's take. Use the AI prompt below. This is the kind of post that gets shared by brand managers and builds Ideoworks' name in the industry.
Why It Works
When Ideoworks has opinions on the industry, brands follow us even when they're not ready to hire an agency yet. So when the moment comes, we're the first name they think of. Sharing a point of view costs nothing: silence is the only mistake.
Where to Get Content
Meta/Instagram/TikTok platform announcements. Local marketing news (Marketeers ID, Campaign Asia). Monthly team check-in: "What's one thing we noticed this month that brands are getting wrong?" That one answer becomes a post.
LinkedIn Posts
• Trend take: "Meta just changed the algorithm. Here's what it means for Indonesian brands." (text post + opinion)
• Myth-bust: "3 things brands still believe about Instagram that are no longer true"
• Data post: "We audited 20 Indonesian brand accounts. Here's what the best ones do differently."
Instagram Posts
• Carousel: "5 signs your agency doesn't understand social media anymore" (controversial hook drives saves)
• Reel: Rapid-fire "fact or myth": text on screen, upbeat music, 30 sec
• Static: Quote from a trend report with Ideoworks' take in the caption
AI Prompt
You are a digital marketing strategist at Ideoworks writing a LinkedIn opinion post. Topic: [e.g., "Meta's new algorithm update" / "Why Indonesian brands still track follower count as a success metric"] Format: - Opening line: bold, specific claim or counterintuitive statement - 3 supporting points, each 1–2 sentences - Closing: what Ideoworks recommends brands do about this - Length: 200–250 words - Tone: confident expert: like a smart colleague sharing an observation over lunch, not an academic paper - Max 3 hashtags. No emojis unless natural. Avoid: "in today's digital landscape", "game-changer", "leverage", "synergy".

Autonomous Agents: AI That Works While You Don't

These tools go beyond answering questions. They take a goal and complete multi-step tasks independently: researching, writing, building, and executing without hand-holding. Think of them as an AI intern that can work overnight.

🤖

Manus (Meta)

Now part of Meta, Manus is the most capable autonomous agent in this stack. Give it a complex task ("audit our competitor's Instagram, summarize their top 10 posts by engagement, and draft 5 post ideas we can steal") and it completes it end-to-end: browsing the web, analyzing content, and writing the output without you steering it. This is what separates Manus from Meta AI (the chatbot): Manus executes, it doesn't just answer. Best for research tasks and multi-step content production.

⚡

Meta AI (WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook)

Meta's own AI assistant, powered by Llama 4. Already available inside WhatsApp and Instagram DMs for Indonesian users. Not an agent (it won't complete tasks autonomously), but useful for quick content ideas and customer query responses directly inside the platforms your team already uses every day.

🔗

n8n / Make.com

Workflow automation platforms that connect AI tools to each other and to your existing stack. Example: when a client result is logged in Notion, automatically trigger Claude to draft a LinkedIn post and save it to a Google Doc for review. Not AI itself, but the connective tissue that makes AI tools work as a system.

🔍

Perplexity (Research Mode)

When given a research question, Perplexity searches the live web, synthesizes sources, and cites every claim. Use it as the research layer before writing: "What are the top 5 digital marketing trends in Indonesia in Q2 2026?" Feed the output into Claude to write the post. Two tools, one clean workflow.
