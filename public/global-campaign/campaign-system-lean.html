HubSpot — What Your Team Does In It

1
Check pipeline every morning (2 min)
Open HubSpot, look at the pipeline board. See where each deal is. Flag anything stuck more than 7 days.
2
Add new leads when they come in (2 min)
When someone replies or books a call: create contact, create deal, assign to stage "New Lead".
3
Set follow-up tasks after every interaction
After each email or call, log a note and set the next task with a date. HubSpot reminds you.
4
Move deals through stages
When a discovery call is done, drag the deal card to "Qualified". When proposal is sent, move to "Proposal". This keeps everyone aligned.
🚀

Apollo.io — How to Use the Free Tier

1

Search by: Job Title (CMO / VP Marketing / Head of Growth), Country (US, UK, Australia, Singapore), Company size (50–500 employees).
2
Export 10–20 contacts per day
Free tier gives 50/month. Use them carefully. Export only people you will actually reach out to that week.
3
Copy email + LinkedIn URL
You'll use email for cold outreach, LinkedIn for connection request. Both from one Apollo profile view.
4
Track who you've contacted
Export Apollo list to Google Sheets. Mark status: Contacted / Replied / Booked. Simple but effective.

What to Fill In Per Deal

Keep it minimal — 5 fields only

1
Company Name + Contact Name
Who are you talking to and where are they from.
2
Deal Value (USD)
Estimate the contract size. Even a rough number. This builds your pipeline forecast.
3
Lead Source
How did they come in? LinkedIn / Cold Email / Referral / Inbound. This tells you what's working.
4
Expected Close Date
When do you think this deal will close? Rough is fine. Helps build monthly forecast.
5
Next Follow-Up Date
Set a HubSpot task. This is the most important field — it stops deals from going cold.

Follow-Up Rules (Simple)

When to reach out and how

1
Same day — respond to inbound
If someone fills a form or replies, respond within 4 hours. Speed here wins deals.
Highest priority
2
Day 3 — if no reply to cold email
Send a 1-line follow-up. "Just checking if this landed." Short. No guilt. Move on after 3 attempts.
3
Day 7 — send value (not a pitch)
Send a relevant case study, insight, or article. No ask. Just useful.
4
After proposal — follow up in 3 days
If they haven't responded to the proposal after 3 days, call or message: "Any questions on the proposal?"
5
No response after 3 tries — park it
Add to a "Nurture" list in HubSpot. Send one email per month with something useful. No pressure.

When to Upgrade Each Tool

Stay free until you hit these specific trigger points. Don't pay for capacity you haven't earned yet.

💡
The rule: Upgrade a tool only when it becomes the bottleneck. If a free tool is doing the job, there is no reason to pay. Free tools here are genuinely good enough for 0–5 clients.
📈

Upgrade Trigger Points

Specific signals that tell you it's time to pay

1
Apollo Free → Apollo Basic ($59/mo) when:
You're hitting the 50 email credit limit AND your outreach is getting a 5%+ reply rate (meaning you've proven the message works and just need volume).
Trigger: 50 credits used in first 2 weeks
2
HubSpot Free → HubSpot Starter ($20/mo) when:
You have more than 2 reps needing access, OR you need automated email sequences so follow-ups happen without manual sending. Around your 3rd–4th client.
Trigger: Team grows past 2 people on CRM
3
Manual alerts → Make.com ($10/mo) when:
You're getting more than 10 leads/week and manually updating HubSpot is taking 30+ minutes daily. Not before then — that manual time forces you to know every lead personally.
Trigger: 40+ leads/month
4
LinkedIn Free → Sales Navigator ($100/mo) when:
You've closed at least 5 clients from LinkedIn, you know it works for you, and you need advanced filtering + InMail to scale. This is a "scale" tool, not a "start" tool.
Trigger: 5+ LinkedIn-sourced clients closed
5
No ads → Google Ads ($5–15/day) when:
You have a proven landing page (5%+ conversion rate confirmed via GA4), and a clear offer that has already closed inbound leads. Run ads only after organic inbound has validated your message.
Trigger: 3%+ form conversion rate from organic
