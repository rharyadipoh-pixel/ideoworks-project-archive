Step 1 — Connect Meta to HubSpot (20 min)

The single most important step. This is where the automation lives.

1
Create a free HubSpot account
Go to hubspot.com/crm — sign up free. Use your company email. No credit card needed.
hubspot.com/crm
2
Go to Settings → Marketing → Lead Capture → Facebook Lead Ads
HubSpot has a built-in Meta Lead Ads connector. You'll log into your Meta Business account and authorize HubSpot to read your lead forms.
⚡ Native integration — no Make.com needed
3
Map your Lead Form fields to HubSpot contact properties
Tell HubSpot which Meta form field = which CRM field. Name → First Name, email → Email, company → Company Name. Done once.
4
Test it — submit a test lead in Meta
Meta has a "Lead Ads Testing Tool" in their developer panel. Submit a fake form. Within 60 seconds, the contact should appear in HubSpot. If it does, you're done.
Where to find it: developers.facebook.com/tools/lead-ads-testing
⚡

Step 2 — Build the Welcome Automation in HubSpot (30 min)

This sends the email and assigns a task automatically the moment a lead comes in.

1
Go to Automation → Workflows → Create Workflow
Choose "Contact-based" and set trigger to: Contact is created AND Lead Source = Facebook
2
Add Action 1 — Send email
Write a short, personal welcome email. See the template below. Include your Calendly link so they can book a call themselves.
Subject: Quick note from IDEOWORKS, [First Name]

Hey [First Name],

Thanks for your interest — I saw you came across IDEOWORKS.

We help global companies grow in new markets through strategy, creative and media.

If now's a good time, you can grab 20 minutes here: [Calendly Link]

Or just reply to this email — happy to answer any questions first.

— Ryan, IDEOWORKS
3
Add Action 2 — Create a deal
HubSpot can auto-create a deal in your pipeline when a contact is created. Set stage to "New Lead" and revenue to your average deal size as a placeholder.
⚡ Automated
4
Add Action 3 — Assign a task to your rep
Create a task: "Follow up with [Contact Name] — Day 3 if no reply". Assign to yourself or your sales rep. Due date: 3 days from today.
⚡ Automated
📅

Step 3 — Set Up Calendly (10 min)

One link. One event type. Put it everywhere.

1
Create a "20-min Discovery Call" event
calendly.com — sign up free, create one event type: 20 minutes. Connect to your Google Calendar. Calendly will only show times when you're free.
2
Add 3 qualifying questions to the booking form
Before they book, ask: (1) What country is your business based in? (2) What's your approximate monthly marketing budget? (3) What's your main growth challenge? This filters serious buyers from window-shoppers.
3
Put this link everywhere
In your HubSpot welcome email. In your Meta bio. In your LinkedIn bio. In every email signature. One link, multiple entry points.

Optimise Your Meta Ad for B2B Lead Gen

Most B2B agencies run Meta ads wrong. Here's what actually works.

✅ What works
✓
Lead Gen Form inside Meta (not website)
Pre-fills name + email. 3× more conversions than sending to a landing page. Use this.
✓
Specific headline over vague one
"We help US brands enter Indonesia" beats "Marketing Agency" every time.
✓
One clear CTA: "Book a Free Strategy Call"
Low commitment offer. Not "Hire Us". Give them a reason to raise their hand.
✓
Narrow targeting: Job Title + Industry
Target CMO, VP Marketing, Head of Growth in your target countries. Small audience, high relevance.
❌ What doesn't work
✗
Boosting posts for B2B leads
Boosted posts are for brand awareness, not lead gen. Use proper Campaigns with Lead objective.
✗
Broad audience targeting
Indonesia + interests = low quality B2B leads. Use Job Title targeting or Lookalike of existing clients.
✗
Sending to a generic homepage
If you're not using Lead Gen Forms, send to a dedicated landing page with one clear offer — not the homepage.
✗
Waiting days to follow up
The automation handles Day 0. But a personal follow-up from a real person on Day 1 is still what closes deals.
