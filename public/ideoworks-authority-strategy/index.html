Pillar 1
Most foreign brands entering Indonesia make the same mistake on day one. They treat it as one market. Indonesia is 270 million people across 17,000 islands, 700 local languages, and consumption patterns that shift dramatically between Jakarta, Surabaya, Medan, and Makassar. A Gen Z consumer in East Java has completely different platform habits, influencer references, and purchase triggers than one in South Jakarta. We have run campaigns here for over a decade across 3,000 clients. The brands that win fastest are the ones who accept this complexity early. The ones who struggle longest try to adapt their Singapore playbook instead of building fresh. Indonesia rewards specificity. It penalizes assumptions. If you are planning an entry here, I am happy to share what we keep seeing on the ground.

Pillar 2
A Singapore client came to us six months ago expecting to hit their Thailand ROAS benchmarks within 60 days of entering Indonesia. We had to have an honest conversation. Indonesia's paid media CPL is 40 to 60 percent lower than Singapore, which sounds like good news. But brand familiarity has to be built from zero. Shopee, Tokopedia, and TikTok Shop each require different creative approaches. COD payment preference still drives checkout behavior in Tier 2 cities. Month one almost always underperforms. Month three is where it clicks. The brands that quit at month two are making a decision based on the wrong data point. Understanding the curve is half the battle. Knowing which levers to pull at each phase is the other half.

Pillar 3
Ramadan in Indonesia is not what most foreign brand briefs treat it as. It is not a holiday season where you add a crescent moon to your creative and increase your budget. It is a 30-day shift in consumer psychology. Daily routines change. Family spending replaces individual spending. Emotional resonance outperforms rational messaging by a wide margin. The two weeks before Eid are the single highest purchase intent window in the Indonesian retail calendar. We have seen international brands run their standard brand awareness approach during Ramadan and get ignored completely. We have seen others lean into the cultural moment and outperform their full-year targets in six weeks. The difference is not budget. It is understanding. If your brand is planning anything in Indonesia between March and May 2027, the planning window is now.

Pillar 4
Three questions we ask every foreign brand before we touch their Indonesia brief. What has already failed? Almost every brand entering Indonesia has tried something that did not work. That failure usually contains more useful information than the brief itself. Who owns the decision locally? Campaigns in Indonesia die not because of bad strategy but because approval chains between regional HQ and local execution are too slow for platform algorithms to optimize. What is the 12-month budget, not the campaign budget? Brands that allocate by campaign rarely build momentum. Those who commit to 12 months compound their returns in a way single campaigns never can. These questions are not gatekeeping. They are the fastest path to a campaign that actually works. Feel free to ask them of any agency you speak to.
